nibble 2022-01-18 18:14
I think another important factor to consider is accountability/transparency when it comes to open source contributions. I really like the idea behind sites like OpenCollective, where not only can it provide an idea of how much has been raised but how funds are being used. - https://opencollective.com/discover Another interesting idea worth mentioning is a business model where organizations/companies pays a subscription to fund open source developers. A benefit behind this model would be orgs getting exclusive access to updates earlier then the general public. This model is meant to assist developers behind more niche projects, such as security audit tools, that are unlikely to get funding as mainstream development projects do. It's been working out for one funding platform ($16k total shared with 5 developers in just 6 months), but it would be interesting to hear your thoughts on subscription-based funding for FLOSS projects in general. - https://porchetta.industries/
