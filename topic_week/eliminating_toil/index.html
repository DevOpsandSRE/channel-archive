Marcus H 2021-12-07 12:43
For me one way to remove toil is to move to a next generation tool eg from Jenkins to Github Actions, from CloudFormation to Terraform - often this means less work after you complete the migration. (edited)
Another way I guard against unnecessary toil is isolating meaningful parts of stack - you separate your provisioning part into one isolated module so it will not intermingle (dependencies) with app module (customer apps) or support app module (prometheus etc). (edited)
this allows you to modify or switch out tech in those isolated modules without affecting other modules, which in turn means less toil (if every change requires change in other modules = a lot of toil). (edited)

The export requires the issue creation and resolution timestamps, the story points, and whatever factors you want to segment the data by ("component" is our most useful factor - telling us which project or API the effort is associated with).
from there you can easily just make a crosstab with standard deviation of time diff between story started and resolved per point estimate
and you can then split that out per component.
For the correlation analysis, you just compare the time diff and the point estimate using the PEARSON function in Excel
