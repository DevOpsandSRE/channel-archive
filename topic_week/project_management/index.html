Eagle 2021-10-04 20:18
But for our project flow we separated our department in several groups (or communities as management calls it) and it has full responsibility over a part of code, including keeping it tested, documented, updated and bug-free (giving support when needed). If some product owner wants a feature it calls for a mission, and we make a plan with all the teams that need to participate (always leaving a couple behind to keep up with the continuous work). When the mission is declared (Designs, architectural plans and timeline) the mission starts. Everybody on the mission has its full focus on the mission, but needs to let others from the community review the changes before merging etc. This makes sure that the community is always accepting the change. Few key points here are: * There is always somebody handling second line of support and fixing bugs (or just tiding) * People working on features don't get interrupted by some high bug or instability, then focus on the mission and the person from previous point has to handle the fan * We can split the teams on what is easy to do instead of splitting them per product or product group * Some more, but it's late here, and I'll answer questions if they arise
