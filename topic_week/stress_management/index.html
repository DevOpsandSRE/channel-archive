Aankhen 2021-09-21 20:38
I don’t know whether this applies to other people too, but a big source of stress for me is the persistent fear that I’ll forget something that’s in my brain right now because there’s just so much to keep track of. Doesn’t matter whether it’s an opinion, a technique, a task, or something else. The best solution I’ve found is writing things down. Not necessarily for anyone else to read—I’m just transferring thoughts from my brain to somewhere else, which means I can free up my bandwidth for other things. The less I need to actively think about, the less pressure I’m putting on myself. I really like the idea of ‘knowledge in the world’ as opposed to ‘knowledge in your head’, which is a more general way of referring to ejecting things from inside your head by encoding them in the world around you: a blog post that summarizes something you just learnt (so you don’t have to relearn it the hard way), a reminder in your calendar (so you don’t have to constantly keep track of when something is supposed to happen), static types in a function (so you don’t have to remind yourself of what you’re supposed to pass to it or expect from it), an empty box on your desk (so you remember to order a new one when you see it the next day)… anything that lightens your mental load.
