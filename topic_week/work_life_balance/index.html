In this age of work from home, it is very important to separate your work space from your living space if at all possible. That way you can "walk away from work".
If your work space is in the same as your living space, it will cause work fatigue and anxiety. It is subtle and will creep up on you.

modulos 2022-01-24 22:51
I had a pretty candid convo with my boss. I told him I wanted complete freedom to come in whenever things needed to get done and flex my time with the projects I'm working on. 3/5 days WFH, 2 days in office, but at my choosing. I told him that if a switch goes down off hours, I can make the decision to either go in that night, get it fixed/replaced or choose to go early the next day to fix it before start of business. "I want to make the hours work for my family and I as best they can."
Sometimes, that is going in at 10PM that night, other times it's 6AM the day of but let me make that decision. All you need to know is that start of day, things are working as normal.
He agreed 100%. it's been the best year ever
If I work after hours/weekends, I flex time. I attend all meetings and during work hours always available on slack. It's been awesome. My energy is up, productivity is high. The point is, take a step back and really think about it. For some people, merging work/home life allows them to balance it all out, disconnect when they can. It's not like that for everyone but it really works for me
Added benefit is that I get to see my non-school aged son during the days I work from home. It's the best thing ever.

I think the topic of work/life balance is also strongly dependant on the part of the world you live at. I know it's different in the USA, but for example in Germany there are quite a lot of laws and regulations (sure, not always upheld by the companies, but generally) to protect the employees. Things like: You worked for 8 hours? Well, you have to have at least a break of 11 hours before you are allowed to begin work again. Not having to be reachable during your off work time and so on. For me it's mostly my own mental disconnection from work. Which is....sometimes difficult because my work topics are also interesting for me. I often look at our company slack during my off hours just because I'm curious and yes, sometimes I do take action because I feel like, even tho, I don't have to. Even our oncall is strictly regulated by a contract, so when I'm not oncall but you try to reach me on my company-mobile phone and I don't answer? Well, tough luck. But sometimes very annoying and tiring things happening at work (often caused by other people!) are the things which I struggle to get out of my head for about a day. Talking and venting to a friend who understands the technical stuff usually helps a lot.
